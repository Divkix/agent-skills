# Sound Symbolism Reference

The phonetic elements of a name influence perception before any conscious processing occurs. Lexicon Branding uses this as a core differentiator. Every generated name should be considered through this lens.

## Vowel Position & Perception

| Vowel Type | Sounds | Symbolic Associations | When to Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Front vowels** | i (ee), ɪ (ih), e (ay), æ (ae) | Smallness, speed, lightness, precision, sharpness | Tech, speed, precision tools, app names, startup names |
| **Central vowels** | ə (uh), ɜr (ur) | Neutrality, approachability, lightness, minimalism | Mass-market, friendly, accessible brands |
| **Back vowels** | u (oo), o (oh), ɑ (ah) | Size, weight, depth, seriousness, openness, generosity | Enterprise, luxury, durability, food, wellness |

### Examples

- **Front vowel names:** Vercel (ɛ), Pixel, Zip, Kip, Swiffer, Hinge, TikTok, Insta, Swift, Febreze, Kinsta
- **Back vowel names:** Toyota, Honda, Corolla, Sonos (o-o), Congo (o-o), Amazon (a), Okta, Turbo, Zoom
- **Mixed front-back:** Sonos (opens soft 'o', closes with sibilant) = both calming and active

## Consonant Classes & Perception

| Consonant Class | Sounds | Symbolic Associations | When to Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Plosives** | p, b, t, d, k, g | Strength, decisiveness, forward motion, impact, power | Sports, performance, enterprise, construction, tools |
| **Sibilants** | s, z, ʃ (sh), ʒ (zh) | Smoothness, softness, sophistication, fluidity, motion | Beauty, wellness, technology, SaaS, premium |
| **Fricatives** | f, v, θ (th) | Precision, airiness, lightness | Clean products, subtlety, medical, fragrance |
| **Nasals** | m, n, ŋ (ng) | Warmth, comfort, resonance, grounding | Food, family, comfort, lifestyle |
| **Liquids/Glides** | l, r, w, j (y) | Flow, openness, playfulness | Friendly, creative, lifestyle, children's |
| **Affricates** | tʃ (ch), dʒ (j) | Controlled energy, activation | Dynamic, youthful, modern |

### Examples

- **Plosive-dominant:** PowerBook, Pentium, Kik, Yelp, TikTok, Puma, BlackBerry, Turbo, Crib
- **Sibilant-dominant:** Swiffer, Sonos, Swish, Salesforce, Cisco, Zest, silky smooth names
- **Nasal-dominant:** Samsung, Amazon, Häagen-Dazs (nasal closure), Yum, Home Depot
- **Liquid-dominant:** Allura, Glow, Lululemon, Lyft, Lucid

## Syllable Count & Rhythm Guidelines

| Length | Effect | Best For | Caution |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1 syllable** | Sharp, decisive, punchy | Apps, tools, action verbs, high tech | Can feel cold or generic if not distinctive |
| **2 syllables** | Balanced, memorable, natural | Most brand names — the sweet spot | Most crowded space; needs distinctiveness |
| **3 syllables** | Melodic, warm, approachable | Services, wellness, food, lifestyle | Any longer feels slow or cumbersome |
| **4+ syllables** | Grand, authoritative | Luxury, heritage, pharmaceuticals | Rarely appropriate for modern tech or consumer |

### The Lexicon "Bar Test":
Say the name out loud as if ordering a drink at a loud bar. Could someone Google it correctly afterward from just hearing it?

## Sound Patterns That Work

### Reduplication (repetition)
Creates rhythm and memorability: Rolo, Coco, Bebe, Lulu, Toto, Poco, Mimo

### Assonance (repeated vowel sounds)
Creates musical cohesion: Google (oo-oo), Zoom (oo), Hulu (u-u), Dropbox (o-o+plosive)

### Alliteration
Works if not cringeworthy: Coca-Cola, PayPal, BlackBerry (when the alliteration is natural and accidental, not forced)

### Palindromes and Symmetry
Highly distinctive: Sonos (reads same upside down and backwards phonetically), Otto, Tutu, Lalala

## Sound Patterns That Fail

### Too Many Plosives In Sequence
"BakPak" — harsh, hard to say, feels aggressive without intent

### Awkward Consonant Clusters
"Fglrrp" — requires articulatory effort, violates "brain is lazy" principle

### Shu-shu Names (Overused Sophistication Trope)
"Shiloh," "Shayla" — common AI naming trap: pretty but empty, indistinctive

### Trend Suffixes (Time-Stamped)
"-ify," "-ly," "-me," "App" in the name, "AI" or "GPT" prefix. These date instantly and dilute distinctiveness.
